Drake Maye finished second in MVP voting at 23 years old. He led the NFL in passer rating (113.5), completion percentage (72.0%), and yards per attempt (8.9). He took the New England Patriots to the Super Bowl in his second season.

Matthew Stafford won the MVP. That's not a knock on Maye - Stafford's season was historically great for a 37-year-old. But Maye was right there.

The question worth pricing: what are the odds Drake Maye wins the MVP before he turns 25?

## Why This Number Isn't Crazy

Maye has two more seasons before his 25th birthday. He gets to play both of them under Bill Belichick's former right-hand man in a division that has, at the moment, no dominant rival at the quarterback position.

The comp that matters here is Patrick Mahomes, who won his first MVP at 23. Lamar Jackson won his first at 24. The league has increasingly recognized young quarterbacks at their peak - the bias toward experience that once held back early-career MVPs has mostly dissolved.

Maye's efficiency numbers in Year 2 are the kind that win MVP awards. He just happened to be in the same year as a 46-touchdown, zero-turnover season from a first-ballot Hall of Famer. That's bad timing, not bad play.

## What Has to Go Right

A Maye MVP before 25 requires roughly this: one more season of top-3 efficiency, a Patriots team that wins 12-13 games, and a field that doesn't have a singular dominant performance from a veteran quarterback.

None of those things are guaranteed. But none of them are unlikely. The Patriots have the infrastructure. Maye has the efficiency. The schedule is manageable.

## What Has to Go Wrong

The most likely scenario where this doesn't happen: Lamar Jackson posts a historic season in Year N+1 and walks away with his third MVP. Jackson's candidacy is the variable most likely to displace Maye regardless of what Maye does.

The second scenario: injury. Maye takes hits. It's part of how he plays. One significant missed game block in 2026 or 2027 ends the conversation.

## The Number

+180 prices a roughly 36% chance he wins at least one MVP in the next two seasons. That feels right. It's not a lock - it requires things to go his way. But it's close to a coin flip with good odds on the upside, which is exactly what you want from a 23-year-old quarterback with the best efficiency season of anyone who didn't win.

The gods set the odds. Now you do.
