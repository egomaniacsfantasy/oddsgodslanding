Maxx Crosby is 28 years old. He plays for the Las Vegas Raiders. Those two facts exist in direct tension with each other.

He is one of the five best defensive players in the NFL. His team has cycled through four head coaches in four seasons. The Raiders have not won a playoff game since the 2002 season.

So: what are the odds that Maxx Crosby wins a Super Bowl before he retires?

  <span className="odds-number">+550</span>
  <span className="odds-label">Implied probability: 15.4%</span>
</div>

## How We Got There

There are roughly two paths to a ring for Crosby.

**Path A:** The Raiders figure it out. They get a legitimate quarterback, a functional front office, and a head coach who survives more than 18 months. This is not impossible - the 49ers did it with Brock Purdy, the Seahawks did it with Sam Darnold. It requires a quarterback, though, and the Raiders don't have one.

**Path B:** Crosby gets traded to a contender. At 28, with a massive contract, this is structurally difficult but not unprecedented. A team like the Eagles or Lions could theoretically absorb his deal for the right return.

We weight Path A at about 12% over the remaining 8-10 years of his career, accounting for the franchise's structural problems. Path B at about 6%, accounting for the contract friction. Combined and adjusted for the overlap, we land at roughly 15-16% - which prices out to about +530 to +580.

We rounded to +550.

## The Uncomfortable Comparison

For context: Myles Garrett is 30. He just broke the single-season sack record. He plays for the Browns, who have had 35 starting quarterbacks since 1999.

Garrett's odds of a ring before retirement: **+1200**. 7.7% implied.

Two of the best defensive players alive. Two of the worst quarterback situations in football. The sport is not always just.

## What Would Move This Number

A Crosby trade to a Super Bowl contender before the 2026 season would push this number close to +220. A second straight year of Raiders dysfunction - new coach, another top-10 draft pick, another reset - pushes it toward +850.

The window is not closed. But it has never been wider, and the Raiders have never been more reliably in the way.
