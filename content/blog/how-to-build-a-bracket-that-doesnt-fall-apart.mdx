Every March, millions of people fill out a bracket. Most of them pick teams they like, sprinkle in one upset to seem smart, and watch it collapse by Thursday night.

The problem isn't the picks. The problem is that most brackets are built as a list of independent guesses, when the reality is that every pick is a *dependency*. Who you pick to win the championship depends entirely on who you think gets out of the East. And who you think gets out of the East depends on whether you believe in the 5-seed from a mid-major or the wounded 2-seed with a star player on a minutes restriction.

This is what the Bracket Lab shows you in real time. But before you open it, here's the mental model.

## Think in Paths, Not Outcomes

The question is never just "who wins the championship." The question is: *what has to go right for this team to win the championship, and how likely is each step?*

A 1-seed's path to the title might run through a particularly brutal region. A 3-seed might have an open road after Round 2 because the top half of their bracket is a mess.

The implied probability of winning the title compounds every round. If a team has a 75% chance of winning each of their six games, their title probability is:

> 0.75⁶ = **17.8%**

That's not an estimate - that's arithmetic. The question is whether 75% per game is the right number.

## The Round of 32 Is Where Brackets Actually Die

Most people focus on their Final Four. Smart bracket builders focus on Round 2.

This is where the 12-over-5 upsets live. This is where the 4-seed you need to beat a 1-seed in the Elite Eight quietly loses to a 13-seed. This is the round that destroys the architecture of your bracket before your Final Four assumptions ever get tested.

When you build your bracket, ask yourself: **what is my 5-seed doing in Round 2?** If you haven't thought about it, your bracket has an unexamined dependency.

## Use the Futures Market as a Reality Check

Sportsbooks set futures odds with better information than you have. That's not a reason to just copy them - it's a reason to understand where you're *disagreeing* and why.

If the market has Duke at +600 to win the title (about 14.3% implied) and you have them in your Final Four, you're effectively betting they overperform their market odds. That's a real bet. You should know you're making it.

The Bracket Lab shows you the current futures-implied odds for every contender, updated in real time as you make picks. If you're making an aggressive pick, you'll see exactly how aggressive.

## The One Pick That Changes Everything

Here's a useful exercise before you finalize any bracket:

Find the one pick where, if you change it, the most other things change. That's your load-bearing pick. It's usually not your championship pick - it's a Sweet 16 or Elite Eight call that opens or closes a path for multiple teams.

Build your bracket from that pick outward. Know what you're betting on. Know what the math says about it.

Then, if you want, ignore the math entirely and pick the team with the best uniforms.

That's also valid. But at least you'll know what you're doing.
